## Overview

This project investigates whether Quantum Machine Learning can outperform classical ML approaches for astronomical image classification tasks. Using galaxy morphology as the primary classification problem, I designed and evaluated quantum circuits for encoding visual features and performing quantum-enhanced classification.

## Research Questions

- Can quantum feature encoding improve classification accuracy over classical CNNs?
- How do variational quantum circuits perform on structured astronomical datasets?
- What are the practical limitations of current NISQ-era quantum hardware for ML?

## Approach

The project follows a structured research methodology — literature survey, experimental design, implementation, evaluation, and documentation. Both Qiskit and PennyLane frameworks were used to implement and compare different quantum circuit architectures.

## Key Findings

Quantum approaches show promise in certain feature encoding regimes but are limited by current NISQ hardware noise and circuit depth constraints. The research provides a clear framework for future investigation as quantum hardware improves.
