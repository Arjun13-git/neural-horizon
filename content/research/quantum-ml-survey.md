## Abstract

This research investigates whether Quantum Machine Learning techniques can provide advantages over classical approaches for astronomical image classification. The primary task is morphological galaxy classification (elliptical, spiral, irregular), with secondary exploration into exoplanet transit detection.

## Motivation

The exponential growth of astronomical datasets from observatories like SDSS and Kepler necessitates automated, accurate classification systems. Quantum-enhanced feature learning may offer advantages in high-dimensional feature spaces typical of astronomical data.

## Methodology

1. Literature survey of QML applications in astronomy
2. Dataset preparation and preprocessing
3. Classical baseline models
4. Quantum circuit design for feature encoding
5. Comparative evaluation
6. Documentation in IEEE format

Active research. Experimental results ongoing. Literature survey completed and presented.
